Introduction & Partnership Overview ### About This Document This Co-Brand Guidelines document governs all joint marketing activities conducted between Employment Hero and ShiftCare in Australia as part of their referral and integration partnership. It establishes how both brands appear together across all touchpoints — from digital campaigns and social media to co-authored content, event collateral, and in-product messaging. These guidelines are designed to protect the integrity of both brands while creating a cohesive, professional, and trust-building experience for the shared audience: Australian disability and aged care providers using ShiftCare for care management and Employment Hero for HR and payroll. ### Our Shared Audience > We both serve small-to-medium Australian care providers — people who are time-poor, compliance-conscious, and looking for software that simplifies their operations. Every co-branded asset should speak directly to their needs, in language they recognise. ### Our Shared Purpose | Employment Hero | ShiftCare | | --- | --- | | To make employment easier and more valuable for everyone. | To simplify the provision of in-home support, helping small businesses grow and deliver a higher standard of care. | | Empowering employers and employees to go further through an Employment Operating System. | Streamlining scheduling, invoicing, and care management to empower providers to raise quality and efficiency. | ### What This Partnership Delivers Together, Employment Hero and ShiftCare offer care providers a fully integrated operations stack: award-compliant payroll, HR automation, and rostering through Employment Hero, paired with ShiftCare's purpose-built care management, scheduling, and NDIS invoicing platform. Co-brand activities should always reinforce this combined value — the whole is greater than the sum of its parts. --- ## 2. Co-Brand Principles Before opening a design file or drafting copy, everyone involved in co-branded activity — on both sides — should apply these five principles. #### 1. Equal Respect Neither brand dominates. Both logos must appear at comparable sizes. Neither brand's colours, typography, or visual language should overwhelm the other. The partnership is between equals. #### 2. Brand Integrity First Each brand must remain recognisable in its own right. Do not attempt to blend, merge, or stylistically unify the two identities into a single new visual language. They sit side by side — they do not become one. #### 3. Clarity Over Cleverness Co-branded assets have one job: clearly communicate a joint value to a busy care provider. Lead with the shared benefit. Keep copy concise and benefit-forward. Avoid internal jargon from either organisation. #### 4. Approval Before Publishing All externally-facing co-branded assets must be reviewed and approved by a designated brand contact at each organisation before publication. Neither party may publish co-branded material that features the other's logo or brand elements without written confirmation. #### 5. People First Both brands share a commitment to portraying the humans in the care sector with dignity, authenticity, and agency. All photography, copy, and design choices must reflect this. People are never reduced to their condition, disability, or role. --- ## 3. Logo Usage ### The Co-Brand Logo Lockup When both brands appear together, the preferred treatment is the horizontal lockup: the Employment Hero logo on the left, followed by a thin separator (an en-dash or × symbol in neutral grey), followed by the ShiftCare wordmark on the right. Neither logo should be larger than the other by more than 10%. ### Employment Hero Logo Rules > **✓ Do** > - Use the approved horizontal lockup as the primary format. > - Apply white logo on dark/coloured backgrounds. > - Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the EH icon. > - Minimum print size: 25mm width. Digital: 140px width. > - Use 'Powered by Employment Hero' badge where EH is the underlying platform. > **✕ Don't** > - Remove any elements from the EH logo. > - Place the EH logo on unapproved colours (e.g. SC coral red as background). > - Apply drop shadows, outlines, or colour fills to the EH logo. > - Change the proportions between the EH icon and wordmark. > - Use the EH favicon independently without brand team approval. ### ShiftCare Logo Rules > **✓ Do** > - Use the navy wordmark on light backgrounds; white wordmark on dark backgrounds. > - Use the standalone 'S' icon only when the full wordmark cannot fit the composition. > - Ensure sufficient contrast between the ShiftCare logo and the background. > - Use only navy, black, or white logo variants. > **✕ Don't** > - Change the ShiftCare logo font or apply effects. > - Use the S icon and wordmark together at the same time. > - Use the S icon where the full wordmark would fit. > - Recolour the logo using non-approved colours (e.g. EH purple on ShiftCare logo). ### Logo Hierarchy by Channel | Asset Type | EH Logo Position | SC Logo Position | Separator | | --- | --- | --- | --- | | Joint landing pages | Top-left | Top-right or beside EH | × in body mid-grey | | Co-branded EDM | Header left | Header right | Vertical rule | | Social tiles | Bottom-left | Bottom-right | × centred | | Event banners | Left of centre | Right of centre | × centred | | Case studies / PDFs | Cover page — left | Cover page — right | × on cover | | EH-hosted web pages | Primary / lead | Secondary, same row | × | | SC-hosted web pages | Secondary, same row | Primary / lead | × | --- ## 4. Co-Brand Voice & Tone ### Where Our Voices Align Employment Hero and ShiftCare share more in common tonally than might first appear. Both brands communicate as a close colleague — warm, intelligent, and helpful without being patronising. Both avoid corporate jargon and prioritise clarity. Both lead with benefit over feature. | EH Voice Traits | ShiftCare Voice Traits | | --- | --- | | Empowering — validates and rallies the audience | Personable — conversational, close-colleague energy | | Transformative — forward-thinking, trailblazer | Impactful — delivers real value, clear and insightful | | Challenger — challenges the status quo, zigs where others zag | Clear — direct everyday language, no jargon | | Emotive, expressive, unexpected | Inclusive — people-first, respectful language | | Short, sharp sentences. Fullstops not exclamations. | Sentence case throughout; benefit-led copy | ### ShiftCare Voice Tone Scales | Dimension | Position | |---|---| | Energy | Upbeat | | Intimacy | Partner | | Attitude | Polite opinionated | | Intelligence | Smart | | Humour | Intelligent wit | ### The Co-Brand Voice #### Energy Upbeat — not manic. Positive and confident, but never hype-y or hollow. Avoid exclamation marks in co-brand copy unless the context genuinely warrants excitement. #### Intimacy Colleague to colleague. Speak as though addressing a competent professional who runs a care business. Acknowledge their expertise. Don't talk down. Don't over-explain. #### Attitude Politely opinionated. We have a point of view — running a care business shouldn't require five disconnected software systems. We make that case clearly, not aggressively. #### Co-Brand Copy Rules Describing the partnership: Refer to the relationship as a 'referral and integration partnership' or 'partnership'. Do not use 'reseller', 'white-label', or 'acquisition channel' in customer-facing copy. Product names: 'Employment Hero' in full on first reference, 'EH' subsequently. Always 'ShiftCare' (one word, capital C). Never 'Shift Care' or 'shift care'. CTAs — co-branded context: Prefer active, benefit-led CTAs. Use: 'See how it works', 'Connect your roster to payroll', 'Book a joint demo'. Avoid: 'Learn more', 'Find out more', 'Click here'. --- ## 7. Photography & Visual Content | Employment Hero Photography Style | ShiftCare Photography Style | | --- | --- | | Photojournalistic — real people in real environments. | Warm, authentic, candid imagery. | | Unfiltered — dirt on hands, sleeves rolled up, lived-in spaces. | Positive situations with carers and loved ones engaged together. | | Dynamic — energy, focus, connection, excitement. | Diverse range of ethnicities; aged care and disability contexts. | | Authentic work attire, no styled wardrobes. | Never cold, clinical, or overly staged. | ### Photography Guidelines for Co-Branded Assets > **✓ Do** > - Use warm, authentic imagery showing care providers doing meaningful work — not staged shots. > - Include a diverse range of ethnicities and abilities. > - Show positive human moments: carer and participant engaged, provider reviewing a roster. > - When showing software, use real in-context shots (tablet in a care home, laptop at a provider's desk). > - Match image energy to the message — educational: considered; campaign: energetic. > **✕ Don't** > - Use cold, clinical stock imagery — sterile environments, generic office workers, posed product shots. > - Reduce people to their disability, condition, or role. Always portray with dignity and agency. > - Feature competitor logos or software in any co-branded image. > - Use generic stock-photo 'handshake' or 'partnership' imagery. > - Place EH 'Hero sticker' elements in ShiftCare-led materials, and vice versa. ### Illustration & Graphic Elements #### Employment Hero graphic elements EH's 'Hero Stickers' and accent lines are EH-brand assets. They may appear in EH-led co-brand materials but should not be placed in ShiftCare-led assets. When used, stickers support the story — they don't overpower the message. #### ShiftCare graphic elements ShiftCare's wave / organic shape backgrounds are SC-brand assets. They may be used in ShiftCare-led assets featuring the EH logo, but should not appear in EH-led materials or neutral joint assets. #### Iconography ShiftCare icons (Care Management, Rostering, Invoicing, ShiftCare App) may be used in joint content when describing ShiftCare features. EH's bold thick-stroke presentation icons may be used in EH-led content. Do not mix icon styles from both brands within a single visual element. --- ## 8. Co-Branded Asset Guidance by Type ### Social Media - Both logos on every slide / frame — not just the cover. - Lead with the audience pain point or benefit. Follow with the partnership proof point. - Tag both brand accounts: @employmenthero and @shiftcare. - Use hashtags: #NDIS #AgedCare #CareProvider #DisabilitySupport alongside brand hashtags. ### Email / EDMs - Subject lines: benefit-led. E.g. 'Automate payroll from your ShiftCare roster' not 'ShiftCare × Employment Hero announcement'. - Both logos in the header. Lead brand logo on the left; partner logo on the right, same row. - One clear CTA per email. Don't split the reader's attention between two separate product demos. - Keep sections visually distinct — SC-coloured section for SC product info; EH-coloured for EH product info. ### Case Studies & Long-Form Content - Cover page: both logos side by side, on a neutral white or light grey background. - Lead with the customer outcome first: e.g. 'Provider X reduced payroll processing time by 6 hours a week by connecting ShiftCare to Employment Hero.' - Approved claims: the integration saves time, reduces manual data entry, and improves payroll accuracy. Do not make performance claims beyond this without data from a real customer. ### Joint Event Collateral - Pull-up banners: both logos at the top; shared tagline and event context; CTA at the bottom. - Slide decks: use the EH template at EH events; SC template at SC events; neutral co-brand cover slide for joint events. - Print materials: minimum logo sizes apply — EH: 25mm; SC: proportional minimum maintaining legibility. ### Integration & In-Product Mentions - Within ShiftCare: refer to Employment Hero as 'Employment Hero payroll integration'. Use the EH horizontal logo (black on light; white on dark). - Within Employment Hero: refer to ShiftCare as 'ShiftCare care management integration'. Use the ShiftCare wordmark (navy on light; white on dark). - Marketplace listings: both logos, one-sentence integration description, clear CTA. Copy to be approved by both brand teams before publishing. --- ## 9. Approval, Governance & Contacts ### Approval Process **Approval Steps:** 1. Asset created by lead brand team 2.
